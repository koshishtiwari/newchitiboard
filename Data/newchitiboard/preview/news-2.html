Sufficient rain leads to quicker plantation

<p>More than 35 percent of the <a href="https://nepalnews.com/s/nation/paddy-planted-without-fertiliser" target="_blank" rel="noopener">paddy</a> plantation has been completed in <a href="https://nepalnews.com/s/travel-and-tourism/bung-and-octopus-waterfalls-in-lamjung-await-tourists" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Lamjung </a>so far. According to the Agriculture Knowledge Center, Lamjung, paddy plantation started in most parts of the district sooner than last year.</p> <p>Chief of Agriculture Knowledge Center Lamjung Sudip Khatiwada said at this time last year, it was difficult to plant an average  of 30 percent. He said that since June this year planting is being done in most places including the fields along the river.</p> <p>According to the Center, some parts of the district are yet to see paddy plantations where the paddy saplings are yet to mature. Khatiwada said that of the eight municipalities in the district highest percentage of the <a href="https://nepalnews.com/s/business/annual-rice-import-touches-50-billion" target="_blank" rel="noopener">plantation </a>was recorded in Marshyangdi and Kwalasothar rural municipalities.</p> <p>Out of 45,050 hectares of cultivable land in Lamjung, paddy is being cultivated in 16,612 hectares. Similarly, Chaite paddy has been cultivated in an area of 910 hectares.</p> <p>Farmers of the district have been planting advanced varieties of paddy like Sabitri, Jethobudho, Ramdhan, Golden Fragrance, Loktantra, Ekle, and other hybrid varieties including Makwanpur, Gorakhnath, Loknath, Mansuli, US 312, etc.</p> <p>Last year, 44,776 metric tonnes of <a href="https://nepalnews.com/s/nation/irrigation-management-encourages-farmers" target="_blank" rel="noopener">paddy</a> were produced in an area of 14,260 hectares in the district.</p> <p> </p>
